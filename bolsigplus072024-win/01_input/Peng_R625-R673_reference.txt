================================================================================
  NOTES
================================================================================

1. The exact matching of R634-R644 and R661-R665 (written generically as
   "e + X -> e + X") to specific vibrational/rotational levels is inferred
   from the threshold energies in the Morgan database, listed in ascending
   order. The Peng paper does not specify the thresholds for these.

2. R632 (e + H + OH -> e + H2O) is the inverse (superelastic) process of
   R631. BOLSIG+ can handle this automatically if the cross section uses
   the "<->" notation for the target/product states.

3. R659 (second N2 ionization) may correspond to ionization from an excited
   state [N2(A) or N2(a) -> N2+] available in the Morgan database, or it
   could be a second ionization channel (e.g., different threshold).

4. R660 in Peng lists "O + O-" (dissociative attachment) while the Morgan
   database labels it as "O2^-". These may represent different channels;
   check the specific cross section data for the dissociative product.

5. For BOLSIG+ input, load cross section files as follows:
   - Morgan.txt:  READCOLLISIONS for H2O (and optionally N2, O2)
   - IST-Lisbon.txt: READCOLLISIONS for N2 and O2
   Avoid double-loading N2 and O2 from both databases.

6. State notation in the paper (Table A1 image):
   - Superscript 3 = triplet, superscript 1 = singlet
   - A, B, C, E, w = standard N2 electronic state labels
   - A' likely = a' (lowercase in spectroscopic notation)
   - "e 3Sigma" (R655) = E 3Sigma_g+ (uppercase E standard)
   - "sing" (R657) = sum of singlet states or singlet dissociation
   - "a 1d" (R666) = a 1Delta_g for O2
   - "b 1s" (R667) = b 1Sigma_g+ for O2
   - "1D" in O(1D) (R670) = excited oxygen atom O(1D)
